The 4 C's of Diamonds

Understanding Cut, Color, Clarity, and Carat Weight - the universal standards for evaluating diamond quality.

Diamond Cut

The cut determines how well a diamond reflects light, affecting its brilliance and fire. A well-cut diamond maximizes light return, creating exceptional sparkle.

Excellent

Maximum brilliance and fire

Very Good

Superior light performance

Good

Excellent value with good brilliance

Diamond Color

Diamond color is graded from D (colorless) to Z (light yellow or brown). The less color, the higher the grade and value.

D-F Colorless
G-J Near Colorless
K-M Faint Yellow
N-Z Light Yellow

Diamond Clarity

Clarity refers to the absence of inclusions and blemishes. Most inclusions are invisible to the naked eye but affect the diamond's brilliance.

FL/IF Flawless/Internally Flawless
VVS1/VVS2 Very Very Slightly Included
VS1/VS2 Very Slightly Included
SI1/SI2 Slightly Included

Diamond Carat Weight

Carat measures a diamond's weight, not size. One carat equals 200 milligrams. Larger diamonds are exponentially more rare and valuable.
